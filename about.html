About 3D Print Cookies

OUR STORY

Who We Are

Decorated cookies are one of the oldest forms of edible craft — and also one of the most inconsistent at scale. A skilled icer can produce fifty perfect cookies. A team of five can produce five hundred of varying quality. 3D Print Cookies applies digital fabrication discipline to confectionery: every batch produced to the same exacting standard as the approved proof, whether the order is 100 or 10,000.

The same ChiLab Studio team behind large-scale digital fabrication projects — including the Uber HQ feature wall and collaborations with Gensler and Knoll — brought that precision-first methodology to the kitchen. We apply parametric design, automated deposition, and process control to a medium most people still produce entirely by hand.

Our Mission

To deliver decorated cookies at a quality level previously only achievable by the most skilled artisan icer — and to do it reliably across batches, deadlines, and production scales that hand-decoration cannot sustain.

Our Vision

A confectionery industry where digital precision and artisan craft are not trade-offs — where the same tools that design buildings and machine parts also decorate the table at the events those buildings host.

OUR VALUES

What Drives Us

🍪

Batch Consistency

Cookie 1 and cookie 500 must be indistinguishable. We run process validation on every design before a production order goes through — because one bad cookie in a corporate gift set is one too many.

◆

Detail Resolution

We're honest about what icing can and cannot hold at cookie scale. If your logo won't resolve at 50mm, we'll tell you before you place the order — and redesign it so it will.

♥

Food Safety

Every substrate is food-safe and produced in an allergen-aware facility. We clearly document every ingredient so your guests can make informed choices — no hidden nut derivatives, no unlabeled dairy.

🆕

Occasion Intelligence

A wedding favor cookie and a conference swag bag cookie are not the same product. We advise on packaging, shelf life, and design complexity based on what the occasion actually demands — not what's easiest for us to produce.
